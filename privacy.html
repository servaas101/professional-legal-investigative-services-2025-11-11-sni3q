Privacy Policy

Last Updated: January 2025

At SOMDA Professional Legal & Investigative Services, we are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring you have a positive experience on our website and when working with our firm. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information.

Introduction

SOMDA Professional Legal & Investigative Services ("we," "us," "our," or "Company") operates the website and provides professional legal and investigative services. This Privacy Policy governs your access to and use of our website, as well as any services we provide to you as a client.

Please read this Privacy Policy carefully. If you do not agree with our policies and practices, please do not use our website or services. By accessing and using our website or engaging our services, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and agree to be bound by all the terms of this Privacy Policy.

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy or our privacy practices, please contact us using the information provided at the end of this document. We are committed to working with you to obtain a fair resolution of any privacy concern.

Information We Collect

Information You Provide Directly

We collect information you voluntarily provide to us when you:

  • Contact us through our website contact forms
  • Request a consultation or legal services
  • Communicate with us via email, phone, or in person
  • Subscribe to our newsletter or mailing list
  • Engage us as your legal counsel or investigative service provider
  • Complete surveys or provide feedback

This information may include your name, email address, phone number, physical address, company information, case details, and any other information you choose to share with us.

Information Collected Automatically

When you visit our website, we automatically collect certain information about your device and browsing activity, including:

  • IP address and device identifier
  • Browser type and version
  • Operating system
  • Pages visited and time spent on each page
  • Links clicked and actions taken on our website
  • Referral source and exit pages
  • Geographic location information

This information is collected through cookies, web beacons, pixel tags, and similar tracking technologies. We use this data to understand how visitors use our website and to improve our services and user experience.

Information from Third Parties

We may receive information about you from third parties in connection with investigations, background checks, or other services we provide. This information is collected only with appropriate legal authority and in compliance with applicable laws.

How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for various purposes, including:

  • Providing legal counsel and investigative services you have requested
  • Communicating with you about your case or engagement with our firm
  • Responding to your inquiries and requests for information
  • Sending you updates, newsletters, and promotional materials (with your consent)
  • Improving and optimizing our website and services
  • Analyzing website traffic and user behavior patterns
  • Ensuring the security and integrity of our website and services
  • Complying with legal obligations and law enforcement requests
  • Protecting our legal rights and the rights of our clients
  • Preventing fraud and maintaining the safety of our website
  • Conducting investigations and gathering evidence as part of our services
  • Billing and payment processing
  • Maintaining client records and case files

We will not use your information for purposes other than those disclosed in this Privacy Policy without first obtaining your consent.

Sharing Your Information

We are committed to maintaining the confidentiality of your information. However, we may share your information in the following circumstances:

  • Service Providers: We may share information with third-party service providers who assist us in providing our services, such as IT support, accounting, and administrative services.
  • Legal Requirements: We may disclose your information when required by law, court order, subpoena, or other legal process.
  • Protection of Rights: We may disclose information to protect our legal rights, privacy, safety, or property, as well as that of our clients and the public.
  • Business Transfers: In the event of a merger, acquisition, bankruptcy, or sale of assets, your information may be transferred as part of that transaction.
  • With Your Consent: We may share your information with third parties when you have explicitly consented to such sharing.
  • Court Proceedings: Information may be disclosed in connection with court proceedings, litigation, or investigative matters where legally authorized.

We maintain strict confidentiality agreements with all third parties who have access to your information and require them to use your information only for the purposes for which it was disclosed.

Data Security

We implement comprehensive security measures to protect your personal information from unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. Our security practices include:

  • Encryption of sensitive data in transit and at rest
  • Secure password policies and authentication protocols
  • Limited access to personal information on a need-to-know basis
  • Regular security audits and vulnerability assessments
  • Employee training on data protection and privacy practices
  • Secure physical facilities and document storage
  • Firewalls and intrusion detection systems

While we strive to protect your information using reasonable security measures, no system is completely secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security of your information. You are responsible for maintaining the confidentiality of any passwords or access credentials you use with our services.

Cookie Policy

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device when you visit our website. They contain information about your browsing activity and preferences. Cookies help us recognize you, remember your preferences, and enhance your user experience.

Types of Cookies We Use

Essential Cookies:

These cookies are necessary for the website to function properly and cannot be disabled. They enable basic functions such as page navigation and access to secure areas.

Performance Cookies:

These cookies collect information about how you use our website, such as pages visited and links clicked. This information helps us improve our website's performance and user experience.

Functional Cookies:

These cookies remember your preferences and choices, such as language preferences and login information, to provide a more personalized experience.

Marketing Cookies:

These cookies are used to track your activity across websites to deliver targeted advertising and marketing messages relevant to your interests.

Managing Cookies

You can control and manage cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to refuse cookies or alert you when a cookie is being sent. However, blocking cookies may affect your ability to use certain features of our website. For more information about managing cookies, please visit your browser's help section or www.allaboutcookies.org.

Third-Party Cookies

Our website may contain links to third-party websites and services that have their own cookie policies. We are not responsible for the privacy practices or cookie usage of third-party websites. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of any third-party websites before providing your information.

Your Privacy Rights

Depending on your location, you may have certain rights regarding your personal information:

  • Right to Access: You have the right to request access to the personal information we hold about you.
  • Right to Correction: You have the right to request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal information.
  • Right to Deletion: You have the right to request deletion of your personal information, subject to legal and contractual obligations.
  • Right to Opt-Out: You have the right to opt-out of receiving marketing communications from us.
  • Right to Data Portability: You have the right to request a copy of your personal information in a portable format.
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: You have the right to withdraw your consent to the processing of your personal information at any time.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the information provided at the end of this Privacy Policy. We will respond to your request within the timeframe required by applicable law.

Retention of Information

We retain your personal information for as long as necessary to provide our services, fulfill the purposes outlined in this Privacy Policy, and comply with applicable legal obligations. The retention period may vary depending on the nature of the information and the purposes for which it is used.

Client files and case information are retained in accordance with legal requirements and professional standards. Investigative records are maintained for the duration of the engagement and may be retained longer if required by law or to protect our legal interests.

When information is no longer needed, we securely delete or anonymize it. However, we may retain certain information for legal, accounting, or administrative purposes as permitted by law.

Children's Privacy

Our website and services are not directed to children under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 18. If we become aware that we have collected information from a child under 18, we will take steps to delete such information and terminate the child's account or engagement with our services.

If you believe we have collected information from a child under 18, please contact us immediately using the information provided at the end of this Privacy Policy.

Our website may contain links to third-party websites and services that are not operated by SOMDA. This Privacy Policy applies only to our website and services. We are not responsible for the privacy practices or content of third-party websites.

We encourage you to review the privacy policies of any third-party websites before providing your personal information. Your use of third-party websites is at your own risk and subject to their terms and conditions.
